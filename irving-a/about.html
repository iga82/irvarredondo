// About //

"Tell me about yourself." This is the first question that every interview I've ever had has started with, and I find it to be the most difficult to answer. It's just so vauge. "Well what do you want to know?" is how I always want to answer. I know they want to hear stuff pertinent to the position I'm interviewing for, so why not just ask "Tell me things about yourself that will want to make me hire you."

My name is Irving Arredondo (if not obvious from the website) and I'm a graduate from the best university in the world, The University of Texas, hook em. I'm currently employed by an amazing company, Workday, where we're changing the game in terms of HR and finanancial management.

I love software. All aspects of it. I can't count how many times I've been asked "front end or back end", and every single time I say "both", which is why I'm constantly doing side projects. With a job, you're restricted to a role, but I love being able to gain experience with a wide variety of technologies. I think the most rewarding feeling in my field is struggling with a technology (ex. setting up the backend) and finally getting it to work. I am sure it's the same feeling that carpenters get when their woodwork is done, it's amazing seeing the puzzle pieces coming together into a finished product.

Outside of software, I'm a sports fanatic. During basketball season, chances are if there is a San Antonio Spurs game on TV, I'm watching it. I'm also a huge Dallas Cowboys fan, and am currently hoping that our usual end of the year slump doesn't happen. I'm currently learning how to play guitar, and my finger tips are already paying the price. I've recently become interested in cooking, which made me buy a couple of cook books, but I'm only allowed to cook healthy meals. In a span of 8 months, I lost 70 pounds through a combination of healthy eating and daily exercise, which are habits that aren't going anywhere. I'm also a huge music fantic, and pride myself over the variety of my music library. I literally love all genres of music from country to classical to alternative and then back to Hip-Hop.

It's hard to summarize myself in 400 words, so if you have any questions about me or just want to talk college football, don't hesitate to contact me.
